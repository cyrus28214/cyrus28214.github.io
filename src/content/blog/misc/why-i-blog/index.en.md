**Hello World!**

It has been 20 days since the Gaokao ended. Amidst the whirlwind of parties, travels, and college applications, I've finally managed to set up this digital space of my own using [Github Pages](https://pages.github.com/) and [Hexo](https://hexo.io/). As an aspiring freshman who has loved computers since childhood, I consider this a coming-of-age gift to myself.

In an era dominated by Weibo, WeChat Moments, and Qzone—where fragmented social media is so ubiquitous—blogging might seem like an ancient relic. Why bother going through the trouble of maintaining a personal blog today?

For me, the answer is simple:

### 1. Building an External Brain

Learning computer science is a long process filled with fragmented knowledge. The bugs I fix today, the environments I configure, and the code I tinker with will likely feel like a foreign language next time if I don't record them.

This blog is, first and foremost, written for "the future me." It is my learning journal and my technical memorandum. When I encounter the same problems again, I want the best solution to be hosted right under my own domain.

### 2. Deliberate Practice of Output

The ability to explain something clearly is incredibly difficult to master. Through consistent writing, I hope to train myself to communicate technical views and logic with precision. Converting cognition into words is, in my opinion, a highly underrated skill.

### 3. Output Forcing Input

There are many technical concepts that seem clear while reading a book, but the moment you try to structure them into a coherent article, you realize how fuzzy your understanding actually is. Blogging forces me to untangle the mess in my head and structure my knowledge. It's essentially a personal application of the "Feynman Technique."

### 4. Seeking Resonance

I hope this blog connects me with like-minded individuals. While its primary purpose is personal growth, it would be a great honor if my learning notes could help someone on the other side of the screen while solving my own problems.

### Final Thoughts

As my university life is about to begin, I don't know how long I can keep this blog updated. But in this summer of 2023, I want to plant a seed.

**"Read widely to accumulate knowledge; give out only a small portion after a long period of preparation."**（博观而约取，厚积而薄发）
